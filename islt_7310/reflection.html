Reflection Page

I tried out a lot of different interests and majors before finding library science for graduate school, and I am pleased that I finally have found the career path for me. I have really enjoyed how library science combines what I love about the humanities and stem. It is a fun challenge balancing the technical aspects and the person-to-person aspects.

While studying library science, I have found a particular interest in archives. I am most interested in digital archives and digital preservation. So much of what we all interact with on a day to day basis is digital, and it is easy to overlook that content in-terms of preservation, given how easy it is to access. I also have loved learning and working with metadata throughout my coursework, it has easily been my favorite topic Iâ€™ve learned about. I love the idea of making content searchable and accessible through metadata.

Currently I am working as an administrative assistant in Washington, D.C. I am hoping over the next few months to involve myself more with archival work at my current job. There are decades worth of documents, pictures, and videos that I would like to help digitize, catalog, and create metadata for. I am also looking for ways to get involved as a volunteer with local museums or historical societies in my area. Overall, I am looking forward to graduation and turning my focus to archives.
